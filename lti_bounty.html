LTI™ App Bounty

or, The Hunt For The Edtech Ecosystem

We’re always on the lookout for great new LTI apps, but right now we want to kick-start things by offering a cash bounty for those who can bring new LTI apps to the up-and-coming edtech ecosystem. Interested? Read on.

Edtech Isn't an Ecosystem

It's sad but true. It turns out ecosystems don’t just happen on their own. Here's a 5-minute presentation that shows what we're trying to do to help grow the edtech ecosystem -- but we definitely can't do it alone.

Video tl;dr:

  • Integrations are super important in edtech
  • Lots of lock-in and anti-social behavior is preventing the edtech ecosystem
  • If any sector should be about openness and interoperability, it should be education
  • LTI is a good step in the right direction, so let's build on that
  • To help grow the cross-platform ecosystem, we launched edu-apps.org
  • Let's open edtech up and see what happens!

Help Us Fix Education

=

If you’re a developer then we want you to help us fix education. There are plenty of products out there with open APIs, SDKs, plugin frameworks, etc. that can improve education, but haven’t yet learned to talk LTI. If you teach one of those products to talk LTI, we’ll give you a reward. If you find a freaking awesome idea and get it to work with LTI we’ll give you an even bigger reward.

We’re serious about this whole edu ecosystem thing, it’s definitely overdue. Help us get the industry to where it should be.

Anyone that creates a qualifying LTI app gets $250.

Top picks from our judges will get an additional $1000.

Submission Criteria

To qualify for the $250 you need to meet these requirements. All apps must be submitted by midnight Mountain Daylight Time on June 10. The submission form is at the bottom of this page.

  • must be a new app (not listed on edu-apps.org. If already listed and you think your change constitutes a significant improvement, let’s talk)
  • must be launch-able as an external tool within the LMS of your choice by June 11th
  • must use standard LTI
  • must include a max-5-minute screencast summarizing your app and showing the LTI integration within an LMS of your choosing
  • must be usable by multiple institutions/users (i.e. it should be possible for someone new to find and install the app)
  • must include directions for how new users can set up the app (i.e. how to retrieve a key and secret, how to configure, etc.)
  • must commit to keeping the app running for at least a year, or open source the code so others can reuse.
  • may use LMS APIs/SDKs, but must at least function using only standard LTI
  • limit one submission per person/team
  • may be a new LTI integration for an existing application/product

Judging Criteria

The best submissions as chosen by the judges will receive an additional $1000. Winners will be announced June 20th. Our judges will review the LTI app submissions and rate them based on the following criteria:

  • user is properly authenticated at app launch (if applicable)
  • app is free of bugs and problems
  • app is easy to understand and use
  • app provides pedagogical value to teachers or students
  • app is innovative or introduces new value

Help is Available!

Questions about building an LTI app? Check out the learning module on LTI, visit the Google+ community or ask a question in IRC (preferrably in that order). Questions about the bounty? Contact @whitmer.

Here are some ideas for cool apps to help get your creative juices flowing:

  • A Dropbox app to link to or embed files from your account.
  • Search and link to resources on Scoop.it
  • Coding app that lets you paste in snippets of code to be syntax-highlight-embedded
  • etc.

Remember, anything that uses LTI and improves education is fair game. There's a lot of potentially awesome LTI apps out there just waiting to be built!
